Translation and Interpretation in Panama

Frequently Asked Questions

Our Services
What services do you offer? ▼
We offer certified Spanish-English translation, legal interpretation (in-person and remote), and apostille-ready document preparation — all delivered digitally at competitive prices.
What types of documents do you translate? ▼
We work with legal documents (contracts, powers of attorney, court records), immigration documents (visas, residency applications), academic transcripts, diplomas, birth/marriage/death certificates, and apostille-ready documents.
Do you offer interpretation services? ▼
Yes. We offer certified legal interpretation for immigration appointments, notary visits, hearings, and private meetings — both in-person in Panama and remotely via Zoom or Teams.
Delivery Times
How long does a translation take? ▼
It depends on the length and complexity of the document. As a general reference: 1–5 pages take 3–5 business days. Rush delivery (24 hours or same day) is available depending on availability and complexity — additional fees apply.
Pricing & Payment
How much does a translation cost? ▼
Rates depend on the document type, length, and requested delivery date. Send your file for a free personalized quote — no commitment required.
How can I pay? ▼
We accept PayPal, Yappy, and bank transfer. Payment is required upfront before delivery. An invoice is issued upon payment confirmation.
Certified Translations
Are your translations certified? ▼
Yes. All certified translations are performed by a Public Translator authorized by Panama's Ministry of Education and Ministry of Foreign Affairs. They include an official signature, seal, and certification statement — accepted by government agencies, courts, and universities.
Do I need to send original documents? ▼
No. For security and efficiency, we work exclusively with digital files. Please send a good-quality PDF or a clear photo via email or WhatsApp.
Other Questions
Is my information confidential? ▼
Absolutely. Confidentiality is a priority. We can sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) upon request.
